## FM 350-1 front connector terminal assignment (S7-300, S7-400)

### Front connector

You connect the following to the 20-pin front connector:

- Count signals
- Digital inputs
- Digital outputs
- Encoder power supply
- Auxiliary voltage and load voltage

> **Note**
>
> The circuits for the counter inputs (encoder supply, encoder signals) are non-isolated from the ground of the CPU. You should therefore connect terminal 2 (1M) to the CPU ground using a low-resistance connection. The lack of such a connection can result in malfunction or a defect in the FM 350-1.

You will find more information on construction and wiring in the [manual](http://support.automation.siemens.com/WW/view/en/1086726) for the FM 350-1 counter module.

### Auxiliary voltage 1L+/1M

Connect a direct voltage of 24 V to the 1L+ and 1M terminals for the 5 V and 24 V encoder voltage supply.

An integrated diode protects the module from a reversal in the polarity of the auxiliary voltage.

The module monitors the connection of the auxiliary voltage.

### 5.2V DC encoder power supply

The module generates a voltage of 5.2 V from the auxiliary voltage 1L+/1M at a maximum current of 300 mA, supplied at the "DC5.2V" connection for the short circuit proof supply of a 5 V encoder. The encoder supply is monitored for short-circuit.

### 24 V DC encoder supply

1L+/1M is provided at output "DC24V" for 24V short-circuit proof encoder voltage supply. The encoder supply is monitored for short-circuit.

### 5 V encoder signals A and /A, B and /B, N and /N

You can connect incremental encoders with 5 V differential signal to the front connector in accordance with RS 422, i.e. incremental encoders with the differential signal A and /A, B and /B, N and /N.

The signals A and /A, B and /B, N and /N are connected via the correspondingly labeled terminals.

The signals N and /N are only connected if you want to set the counter to the zero mark of the encoder.

The inputs are not isolated from the S7-300 bus.

### 24 V encoder signals A*, B* and N*

24 V signals are identified with the letters A*, B* and N*.

You can connect three different encoder types to each counter:

- Incremental encoder with 24 V signals:

  The signals A*, B* and N* are connected via the correspondingly labeled terminals.
- Pulse encoders without direction level:

  The signal is connected to terminal A*.
- Pulse encoders with direction level:

  The count signal is connected to terminal A*. The direction level is connected to terminal B*.

The inputs are not isolated from the S7-300 bus.

### Input filter for 24 V encoder signals

To suppress faults, you can configure input filters (RC elements) with a uniform filtering time for the 24 V inputs A*, B* and N*. The following two input filters are available.

| Characteristics | Input Filter 1  (default) | Input Filter 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Typical input delay | 1 μs | 15 μs |
| Maximum count frequency | 200 kHz | 20 kHz |
| Minimum pulse width of the count signals | 2.5 μs | 25 μs |

### Digital Inputs DI Start, DI Stop and DI Set

You can use digital inputs DI Start and DI Stop for the gate control of the counter. The gate control can be either level-controlled or edge-controlled.

Digital input DI Set is used to set the counter to the load value.

The digital inputs are operated with a nominal voltage of 24 V.

The digital inputs are isolated from the bus of the S7-300 and the counter inputs.

### Input Filters for Digital Inputs

For the purpose of suppressing interference, you can parameterize input filters DI-Start, DI-Stop and DI-Set (RC elements) with a uniform filter time for digital inputs. The following two input filters are available:

| Characteristics | Input Filter 1  (default) | Input Filter 2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Typical input delay | 1 μs | 15 μs |
| Minimum pulse width of the input signals | 2.5 μs | 25 μs |

### Digital Outputs DO0 and DO1

The FM 350-1 has two digital outputs, DO0 and DO1, for directly triggering control processes.

The digital outputs are supplied via the load voltage 2L+.

The digital outputs are isolated from the bus of the S7-300 and the counter inputs.

The digital outputs are Sourcing outputs and can carry load current of 0.5 A. They are protected from overload and short-circuit.

> **Note**
>
> Relays and contactors can be connected directly without external circuitry.

The time response of the digital outputs depends on parameter assignment.

### 2L+/2M load voltage

For supplying digital outputs DO0 and DO1, a load voltage of 24 V must be supplied to the module via terminals 2L+ and 2M.

An integrated diode protects the module from polarity reversal of the load voltage.

The load voltage 2L+/2M is not monitored by the FM 350-1.

#### Selecting an operating mode (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

You define counter module functionality by setting an operating mode.

| Operating mode | Description |
| --- | --- |
| [Count continuously](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#count-continuously-s7-300-s7-400)  (with or without gate) | The FM 350‑1 counts continuously from the current counter value. |
| [Count once](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#count-once-s7-300-s7-400)  (with hardware or software gate) | When the gate opens, the FM 350‑1 counts from the load value to the count limit. |
| [Count periodically](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#count-periodically-s7-300-s7-400)  (with hardware or software gate) | When the gate opens, the FM 350‑1 counts from the load value between the count limits. |
| [Frequency measurement](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#frequency-measurement-s7-300-s7-400)  (with hardware or software gate) | The FM 350‑1 counts the pulses that occur during a dynamic measuring time. |
| [Rotational speed measurement](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#rotational-speed-measurement-s7-300-s7-400)  (with hardware or software gate) | The FM 350‑1 counts pulses received from a tachometer generator within a dynamic measuring time and calculates the velocity based on this value and the number of pulses per encoder revolution. |
| [Period measurement](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#period-measurement-s7-300-s7-400)  (with hardware or software gate) | The FM 350‑1 indicates the dynamic measuring time as a period. The mean value of the period is calculated if its duration is less than the update time. |

The default setting is the "Count continuously" operating mode.

#### Specifying the high count limit (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

By configuring an high count limit together with a [main count direction](#selecting-main-count-direction-s7-300-s7-400)  (up or down), you can reduce the maximum counting range. The parameterized counting range is then between 0 and the high count limit parameterized.

The high count limit can be any value between > 1 and the high count range limit.

#### Specifying measuring time (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

If you have selected a measuring mode, the module will update the measured values on a cyclic basis. The update time is the product of the measuring time x 10ms. You set the measuring time as the factor n.

Detailed descriptions of the value ranges for the measuring time can be found under "[Measuring basics](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#measuring-basics-s7-300-s7-400)".

#### Selecting gate control (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

You define the count process in the selected operating mode by setting a gate control.

- without gate

  All gates are open.
- [Software gate](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#software-gate-s7-300-s7-400)

  The software gate is opened and closed by the input parameter / control bit SW_GATE.
- [Hardware gate](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#hardware-gate-s7-300-s7-400), level-controlled or edge-controlled

  The hardware gate is opened and closed by the corresponding signals at the digital inputs.
- [Latching](#latching-s7-300-s7-400)

  You can save (latch) the counter values by generating edges at the digital input "DI start".
- [Latch/Retrigger](#latchretrigger-s7-300-s7-400)

  You can save (latch) the counter values by generating edges at the digital input "DI start". The counter is set to the load value each time the values are saved.

The default setting is "Without gate".

#### Latching (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

If you select "Latch" for the gate control, you can save (latch) the counter values with edges at the "DI start" digital input. This does not change the counter value.

##### Requirements

You must use the [software gate](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#software-gate-s7-300-s7-400) and select the [edge for latching](#selecting-an-edge-for-latching-s7-300-s7-400).

The minimum interval between latch edges is 1 ms. Values can be lost if the time interval between edges is less than this value.

##### Function principle

The counter and latch values are assigned an initial value. The initial value is 0 for operating modes without a main count direction and for main count direction up. For main count direction down, the initial value is the parameterized high count limit.

The counting function is started when the software gate is opened. The counter starts at the load value.

The latch value is always exactly equivalent to the counter value at the time the edge trigger is generated.

The "DI start" value is indicated in the DB at status bit STS_STA.

The latch value is indicated by LATCH_LOAD in the DB.

The diagram below shows an example of "Latching" when load value = 0 and there is a positive edge at "DI start".

![Function principle](images/17868511755_DV_resource.Stream@PNG-en-US.png)

##### Interrupting and exiting

Closing the software gate by resetting SW_GATE will cancel counting.  
The unchanged counter value can be resaved with edges at "DI start" even if the software gate is closed.

However, the count will be cancelled if you close the software gate by executing GATE_STP of instruction CNT_CTL1. "DI start" can in this case no longer be used to save any counter values.

##### Hardware interrupt upon "Latching"

A hardware interrupt can be triggered each time a counter value is saved by "Latching". As a consequence, you may need to increase the interval between the edges. The hardware interrupts will be lost if the interrupt rate is higher than the system acknowledgement rate. This situation is reported by a diagnostic interrupt.

#### Latch/Retrigger (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

If you select "Latch/Retrigger" gate control, you can save (latch) the counter values with edges at the "DI start" digital input. After each latching operation, the counter will be initialized with the load value, and resumes the count starting at the load value (retrigger.)

##### Requirements

You must use the [software gate](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#software-gate-s7-300-s7-400) and select the [edge for latching](#selecting-an-edge-for-latching-s7-300-s7-400) for "Latch/Retrigger".

The minimum interval between latch edges is 1 ms. Values can be lost if the time interval between edges is less than this value.

##### Function principle

The counter function is enabled by opening the SW gate.

The counter and latch values are assigned an initial value. These values do not change by opening the SW gate. The initial value is 0 for operating modes without a main count direction and for main count direction up. For main count direction down, the initial value is the parameterized high count limit.

The first edge at input "DI start" starts the count with the load value.

The load value is loaded again at each further edge at the input "DI start".

The latch value is always exactly equivalent to the counter value at the time the edge trigger is generated.

The "DI start" value is indicated in the DB at status bit STS_STA.

The latch value is indicated by LATCH_LOAD in the DB.

The diagram below shows an example of "Latch/Retrigger" when load value = 0 and there is a positive edge at "DI start".

![Function principle](images/17868622987_DV_resource.Stream@PNG-en-US.png)

[Measuring times between two edges](#measuring-times-between-two-edges-s7-300-s7-400) using the [internal time base 1 MHz](#internal-timebase-1-mhz-s7-300-s7-400) is a special application for the "Latch/Retrigger" gate control.

##### Interrupting and exiting

Closing the software gate by resetting SW_GATE will interrupt counting, i.e. the count will continue when the software gate is reopened.  
The unchanged counter value can be resaved with edges at "DI start" even if the software gate is closed.

However, the count will be cancelled if you close the software gate by executing GATE_STP of instruction CNT_CTL1. "DI start" can in this case no longer be used to save any counter values.

##### Hardware interrupt upon "Latch/Retrigger"

A hardware interrupt may be triggered each time a counter value is saved by executing "Latch/Retrigger". As a consequence, you may need to increase the interval between the edges. The hardware interrupts will be lost if the interrupt rate is higher than the system acknowledgement rate. This situation is reported by a diagnostic interrupt.

#### Selecting the counting range (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

There is a 32-bit wide count register on the module. With the Count range, you select whether the module is to count only in the positive range or whether the 32nd bit is interpreted as a sign bit thus allowing negative numbers to be represented. You can only select a count range if no main counting direction is set.

Select the desired counting range.

For more information, please see "[Counting ranges and count limits](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#counting-basics-s7-300-s7-400)".

##### Violation of the high limit, low limit and zero pass

A bit is set in the DB of the CNT_CTL1 instruction for both count limits when the high or low limit is violated.

A bit is also set in the DB in the "-31 to +31-bits" counting range in the event of a zero pass.

Depending on the count direction, violation of the high or low limit is also indicated in the "0 to +32 bits" counting range upon zero pass.

| Event | Status bit in DB |
| --- | --- |
| Violation of the high limit | STS_OFLW is set. |
| Violation of the low limit | STS_UFLW is set. |
| Zero pass | STS_ZERO is set. |

##### Initiating Hardware Interrupts

You can also signal the violation of the high limit, low limit and the zero pass events using hardware interrupts.

#### Selecting the gate function (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

When configuring the hardware and software gates, you can specify whether the internal gate is to cancel or interrupt counting.

- [Cancel counting](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#cancel-and-interrupt-function-of-the-gate-s7-300-s7-400): The counting process restarts from the beginning after gate stop and restart.
- [Interrupt counting](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#cancel-and-interrupt-function-of-the-gate-s7-300-s7-400): The counting process starts from the last current counter value after gate stop and restart.

#### Selecting main count direction (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

By configuring a [main count direction](Counting%20and%20measuring%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#main-count-direction-s7-300-s7-400) (up or down), you can reduce the maximum counting range by setting an [high count limit](#specifying-the-high-count-limit-s7-300-s7-400). The parameterized counting range is then between 0 and the high count limit parameterized.

A main count direction can only be selected in the "Count once" and " Count periodically" modes.

You can select the following behavior:

- None (no main count direction)
- Up
- Down

#### Selecting an edge for latching (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Selecting edges

You can select the edge at the digital input "DI start" at which the counter values are to be saved (latched). The following behavior can be configured:

- Latching at a positive edge at "DI-Start"
- Latching at a negative edge at "DI-Start"
- Latching at both edges at "DI-Start"

#### Selecting period resolution (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Description

The value of the calculated period is displayed in 1 μs or 1/16 μs.

Select the required unit of resolution.

##### Possible Measuring Ranges with Error Indication

- The absolute error for a **resolution of 1 µs** is equal to 0.1‰ of the measured value.
- The table below sets out the measuring ranges and errors for a**resolution of 1/16 µs**:

  Period T<sub>min</sub> ± Absolute error

  1/16 μs x (160 ± 1)

  1/16 μs x (1,600 ± 1)

  1/16 μs x (16,000 ± 3)

  1/16 μs x (160,000 ± 20)

  The absolute error for a period of 100ms or more is equal to 0.1‰ of the measured value.

##### Setting the counter (S7-300, S7-400)

###### Overview

If you want to start the count from a specific value, the so-called load value, you must define the signal that is to be used to set the counter to the load value. You can set the counter:

- With the L_DIRECT or L_PREPAR input parameter of the CNT_CTL1 instruction
- With an external signal, using either the "DI set" input or "DI set" in combination with the zero mark of the encoder

###### Load value

Any number within the counting range can be specified as the load value.

The load value is interpreted according to the selected counting range. If, for example, you specify FFFF FFFF H as the load value, this will be interpreted as 4 294 967 295 in the "0 to +32 bits" counting range and as -1 in the count range "-31 to +31 bits".

Enter the load value in the DB of the CNT_CTL1 instruction and transfer it to the module with CNT_CTL1.

The following ranges of values apply for the load value:

| Value range for load values | Main count direction |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| None | Up | Down |  |
| Low limit | Maximum low count limit | -2<sup>31</sup> + 1 | 2 |
| High limit | Maximum high count limit | Parameterized high count limit - 2 | 2<sup>31</sup> - 1 |

###### Setting the counter with an external signal

You can select two different external signals with which to set the counter to the load value:

- ["DI set"](#setting-the-counter-with-di-set-s7-300-s7-400) only
- ["DI set" and zero mark](#setting-the-counter-with-di-set-and-zero-mark-s7-300-s7-400) of the encoder

You use the encoder zero mark if you want to synchronize the counter to a specific counter value at a specific point in your process. This improves the precision of the counting process.

The counter is set independently of the counting mode.

When the counter has been set with an external signal, bit STS_SYNC will be set in the DB.

> **Note**
>
> Synchronizing a counter with the zero mark only makes sense if the gate is open.
>
> If you have only enabled one count direction when setting a counter with an external signal, you must note that the current count direction will be saved (frozen) when the gate is closed. This allows the counter to be synchronized against the enabled count direction.

##### Setting the counter with "DI set" (S7-300, S7-400)

###### Setting the counter with "DI set"

The counter can be loaded with the load value by means of a rising edge at "DI set".

You can define the response of the FM 350‑1 to a rising edge at DI set with variables ENSET_UP and ENSET_DN in the DB of the CNT_CTL1 instruction and by assigning parameters.

| Parameter | Behavior of the FM 350‑1 |
| --- | --- |
| ENSET_UP set | The counter is only set if counting up. |
| ENSET_DN set | The counter is only set if counting down. |
| ENSET_UP and ENSET_DN set | The counter is set for counting up and counting down. |
| "Single setting of counter" parameter assignment | The counter is set only in response to the first rising edge at "DI set".  If the counter is to be set again, you must first set ENSET_UP or ENSET_DN again. The counter will then be set again at the next rising edge at "DI set". |
| "Multiple setting of counter" parameter assignment | The counter is set in response to each rising edge at "DI set" as long as ENSET_UP and/or ENSET_DN are set. |

> **Note**
>
> It is imperative that you set one of the two variables ENSET_UP and ENSET_DN so the counter can be set by the digital input "DI set".

#### Measuring times between two edges (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Introduction

You can measure the time intervals between two successive edges at the "DI start" digital input.

##### Requirements

Ensure the following conditions are met before measurement:

- Do not connect any encoders to the FM 350‑1.
- Configure a [counting mode](#selecting-an-operating-mode-s7-300-s7-400) under operating modes.
- Configure the ["Latch/Retrigger"](#selecting-gate-control-s7-300-s7-400) gate control under operating modes.
- Configure the signal type ["Internal time base 1 MHz"](#selecting-the-encoder-signal-type-s7-300-s7-400) under encoder.

##### Selecting edges

| To measure the time between two immediately successive ... | ... Parameterize |
| --- | --- |
| Rising edges at "DI start" | Latch with positive edge |
| Falling edges at "DI start" | Latch with negative edge |
| Any edges at "DI start" | Latch with both edges |

See also: [Selecting an edge for latching](#selecting-an-edge-for-latching-s7-300-s7-400)

##### Function principle

The FM 350‑1 uses an internal time base 1 MHz to measure times. The time measurement starts upon the first edge at "DI start". At every subsequent edge at "DI start", the time in µs that has elapsed since the last edge was received is saved as the latch value LATCH_LOAD in the feedback interface.

##### Comparison values and outputs (S7-300, S7-400)

###### Description

You can store two comparison values (Comparison value 1 and 2) in the FM 350‑1. These comparison values are assigned to the two digital outputs (Comparison value 1: DO0, Comparison value 2: DO1). The comparison values are compared with the current counter value. When the counter value reaches a comparison value, the relevant output can be set.

Both comparison values are assigned to DO1 in "[Switch at comparison values](#switch-at-comparison-values-s7-300-s7-400)".

###### Comparison values 1 and 2

You enter the two comparison values in the DB of the CNT_CTL1 instruction (CMP_V1, CMP_V2) and transfer them to the FM 350‑1 by setting the bits T_CMP_V1 or T_CMP_V2. This does not affect counting.

The comparison values must be within the limits of the selected counting range. The comparison value is interpreted according to the selected counting range. If you specify FFFF FFFF H as the comparison value, for example, this value will be interpreted as 4,294,967,295 in the "0 to +32 bits" counting range and as -1 in the"-31 to +31 bits" counting range.

The following ranges of values are permitted for the comparison values:

| Value ranges for comparison values | Main count direction |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| None | Up | Down |  |
| Low limit | Maximum low count limit | -2<sup>31</sup> | 1 |
| High limit | Maximum high count limit | Parameterized high count limit -1 | 2<sup>31</sup> - 1 |

###### Enabling the outputs

Before the outputs can be set, you must first enable them by setting the relevant bits in the DB. If you reset one of these bits, the associated output will be switched off immediately even if you have configured a pulse duration for it.

The following bits must be set to enable the outputs:

| Output | ... is enabled by enable bit |
| --- | --- |
| DO0 | CTRL_DO0 |
| DO1 | CTRL_DO1 |

###### Setting and resetting the outputs

If you have configured "No comparison" for output behavior, you can set and reset enabled outputs using the corresponding bits in the DB.

Outputs are set and reset with the following bits:

| Output | ... is set by | ... is reset by |
| --- | --- | --- |
| DO0 | SET_DO0 = 1 | SET_DO0 = 0 |
| DO1 | SET_DO1 = 1 | SET_DO1 = 0 |

#### Specifying pulse duration (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Pulse duration

You can set a pulse duration in order to adapt to the actuators (contactors, actuators, etc) used in your process. The pulse duration indicates how long outputs DO0 and DO1 are active on reaching a comparison value.

The pulse duration only affects the main count direction when "Main count direction up" or "Main count direction down" has been assigned.

The pulse duration can affect both main count directions if a main count direction has not been parameterized.

The pulse duration starts when the output is set. The pulse duration inaccuracy is less than 1 ms.

A value between 0 and 500 ms may be set for the pulse duration. This value applies to both outputs.

If the pulse duration = 0 ms, the output is set when the comparison value is reached and is reset at the next count pulse.

The value 0 is set by default for the pulse duration.

> **Note**
>
> If you specify pulse duration = 0 ms, the output will remain active as long as the counter value equals the comparison value (see top half of figure below).
>
> Control pulses may be lost at the outputs if the time interval between the count pulses is less than the switching times of the digital outputs (up to 300 µs) (see bottom half of figure below).
>
> You should therefore make sure that the interval between the count pulses is greater than the switching times of the digital outputs.

#### Setting hysteresis (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Function principle of the hysteresis

An encoder can stop at a certain position, and then "oscillate" around this position. This state causes the counter level to fluctuate around a certain value. If there is a comparison value within this fluctuation range, for example, the associated output would be switched on and off in time with these fluctuations. The FM 350‑1 has configurable hysteresis to prevent this switching in response to small fluctuations.

A value between 0 and 255 can be set for hysteresis.

The effect of hysteresis is as follows in accordance with the value parameterized:

| Hysteresis | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Hysteresis value n = 0, 1 | The hysteresis has no effect (switched off)  The output reacts to the slightest change in the counter value. |
| 2 ≤ Hysteresis value n ≤ 255 | The hysteresis takes effect.  The output does not react until the counter value is offset by n units to the comparison value. |

The hysteresis applies to both high limit violated and low limit violated.

#### Enabling hardware interrupt trigger (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Introduction

The FM 350‑1 allows you to set which events are to trigger a hardware interrupt during operation.

##### What is a Hardware Interrupt?

If you want to set a response to a specific event independently of the CPU cycle, the FM 350‑1 can initiate a hardware interrupt. The CPU interrupts the cyclic program on receiving the interrupts and executes the hardware interrupt OB.

##### Enabling the Hardware Interrupt

Hardware interrupts are enabled in two steps:

1. When configuring the hardware, you enable the interrupts for the module in the basic parameters and choose whether the module is to trigger diagnostic and/or hardware interrupts.
2. You then select the relevant events under "Enable hardware interrupts":

   - [Events to trigger a hardware interrupt in counting modes](#events-to-trigger-a-hardware-interrupt-in-counting-modes-s7-300-s7-400)
   - [Events to trigger a hardware interrupt in measuring modes](#events-to-trigger-a-hardware-interrupt-in-measuring-modes-s7-300-s7-400)

##### Hardware Interrupt OB, OB 40

If a hardware interrupt occurs, the user program is interrupted, the data is transferred from the module to the [start information of OB40](#ob-40-start-information-s7-300-s7-400) and OB40 is called. The hardware interrupt is acknowledged by exiting OB 40.

If no OB 40 is programmed, the CPU goes into STOP mode. If you then switch back to RUN, the hardware interrupt requirements are deleted.

##### Lost hardware interrupt

If an event occurs that is to initiate a hardware interrupt and the same previous event has not yet been acknowledged, no further hardware interrupt is initiated; the hardware interrupt is lost. This may lead to the "Hardware interrupt lost" diagnostic interrupt, depending on the parameterization.

##### Default setting

No hardware interrupts are enabled in the default setting.

## Using FM 350-1 from the user program (S7-300, S7-400)

### Integrating the FM 350-1 into a user program

Instructions are available for integrating the FM 350-1 into a user program. These enable you to handle the desired functions with ease.

The instructions CNT_CTRL, CNT_CTL1 and CNT_CTL2 can be called in the CPU user program. They ensure communication between the CPU and the FM 350-1.

All data associated with a channel of the module are in the DB of the instruction CNT_CTRL, CNT_CTL1 or CNT_CTL2 ([counter DB](FM%20x50-1%20Counting%20%28S7-300%2C%20S7-400%29.md#counter-data-block-s7-300-s7-400)). Use the command "Program blocks > Add new block" to add a CNT_CHANTYPE1 type data block to your project.

The DIAG_INF instruction is also available for FM 350-1. This operation allows you to transfer the diagnostic information to the counter DB.

The instruction CNT_CTL2 is only used in isochrone mode.

#### Triggering diagnostics interrupts (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Definition of a diagnostics interrupt

You can determine reactions to internal or external errors in the user program by configuring a diagnostic interrupt which interrupts cyclic program execution on the CPU and calls diagnostic interrupt OB.

##### Events which can trigger a diagnostics interrupt

The following events can trigger a diagnostic interrupt:

- Faults in the external auxiliary 1L+/1M voltage.
- 5.2 V DC encoder supply short circuit or overload
- No module configuration
- Faulty module parameters
- Time monitoring (watchdog) triggered
- RAM defective
- Hardware interrupt lost
- Signal A error (wire break, short-circuit, cable missing)
- Signal B error (wire break, short-circuit, cable missing)
- Signal N error (wire break, short-circuit, cable missing)
- Coding plug incorrectly connected

##### Enabling diagnostics interrupts

When configuring the hardware, you enable the interrupts for the module in the basic parameters and choose whether the module is to trigger diagnostic and/or hardware interrupts.

##### Reactions to a diagnostics interrupt

When an event occurs that could trigger a diagnostic interrupt:

- The diagnostics information is stored in the [diagnostic data records DS0 and DS1](#diagnostics-data-record-ds0-and-ds1-s7-300-s7-400) in the module.
- The group error LED is lit.

  The group error LED does dark when the error is eliminated.
- The diagnostic interrupt OB (e.g. OB 82) is called.
- The diagnostic data record DS0 is entered in the start information of the diagnostic interrupt OB.
- The count continues unchanged if a diagnostic interrupt OB has been programmed. If no diagnostic interrupt OB has been programmed, the CPU goes to STOP.

##### Default setting

No diagnostic interrupts are enabled in the default setting.

#### Diagnostics data record DS0 and DS1 (S7-300, S7-400)

##### Diagnostic data record DS0 and DS1

The information showing the event which has triggered a diagnostics interrupt is written to the diagnostics data records DS0 and DS1. The diagnostic data record DS0 comprises four bytes. DS1 comprises 16 bytes; the first four bytes are identical to DS0.

##### Reading data records from the module

[Diagnostic data record DS0](#assignments-of-diagnostics-data-record-ds0-s7-300-s7-400) is automatically transferred to the start information when the diagnostic OB is called. These four bytes are written to the local date (bytes 8 to 11) of the diagnostic interrupt OB.

You can read out the [diagnostic data record DS1](#assignment-of-diagnostic-data-record-ds1-s7-300-s7-400) (and thus also the content of the DS0) from the module with the instruction DIAG_INF. However, this is only useful if DS0 reports a channel error.
